I recently reviewed cyber insurance quotes for a client. The requirements have changed a lot, and most small businesses are not ready for them.

## What insurers want now

It is no longer a checkbox form. Carriers want proof. The common requirements I see:

- Multi-factor authentication on email and remote access, with no exceptions.
- Endpoint detection and response, not just antivirus.
- Tested, offline backups.
- A written incident response plan.
- Security awareness training for staff.

## What this means for you

If you cannot show these, you either get denied or you pay a much higher premium. The good news is that most of them are things you should be doing anyway.

Treat the insurance application as a free security audit. It tells you exactly where the gaps are. Close them, and you lower both your risk and your premium at the same time.
