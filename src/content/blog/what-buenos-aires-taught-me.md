Back in 1992 my mother, my brother, and I started an IT company out of my garage. Soon it took over the whole house. The living room was the sales office. The team ate in the kitchen every day. Our bedrooms held the tables where we built computers.

## Resourcefulness by necessity

Argentina teaches you to solve problems with what you have. You move fast, and you do not wait for perfect conditions. When the rules change overnight, you adapt or you close.

That is the same muscle I use running an MSP in Chicago. The context is different, but the instinct is the same: figure it out, take care of the client, and keep going.

## The one lesson that stuck

Clients remember how you make them feel. The technology matters, but the relationship is what lasts. I learned that in a garage in Buenos Aires, and it is still true on the North Shore.
