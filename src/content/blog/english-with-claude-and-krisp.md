English is not my first language, and I do not pretend it is. What I do is treat it like any other business problem: measure it, find the highest-leverage weakness, and work on that one thing.

## The workflow

Krisp records and transcribes my meetings. At the end of the week I take those transcripts and run them through Claude with a simple prompt: find the patterns I repeat, the verb tenses I miss, and the words I reach for when I am stuck.

That gives me a short list. Not fifty things to fix. Three or four. I drill those until they stop showing up in the next batch of transcripts.

## Why it works

Most language advice is generic. This is specific to me, because it comes from how I actually talk in real meetings, not from a textbook. The feedback loop is short and the data is real.

It is slow, and that is fine. I am getting better all the time, and I can see it in the numbers.
